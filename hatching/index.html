FAIRY SHRIMP & MOINA HATCHING INSTRUCTIONS

KEEP WATER TEMPERATURE BETWEEN 75–80°F DEGREES

Build your hatchery

Items you’ll need:

  • A lamp or similar direct light heat source
  • 3/16″ Rigid Airline Tubing (Length: 12″)
  • Two Water Bottles

Step 1

Cut bottles along the lines. You can repurpose the water inside the bottles to hatch your moina and fairy shrimp eggs. If using tap water, use a water conditioner to remove chlorine. These are freshwater invertebrates. DO NOT USE AQUARIUM SALT!

Step 2

Assemble hatchery:

  • Place one bottle upside down inside the other. The bottom one serves as the base. Fill with 1/3 spring water to keep stable.
  • Connect flexible air tubing to the air pump, then to the rigid airline tubing.
  • Insert rigid tubing into the hatchery and turn the pump on.

Step 3

Prepare and hatch the eggs:

  • Warm eggs under sunlight or a lamp for 2–4 hours. Then soak in spring or dechlorinated freshwater for 8–24 hours.
  • Oxygenate the water using an air stone. Turn on the lamp directly above the hatchery. Keep the light on until eggs hatch.
  • Fairy shrimp hatch in 1–2 days. Moina in 3–10 days (sometimes up to 16). Feed twice daily using yeast or spirulina, small amounts only.
  • Maintain water level by topping off with spring water. The culture naturally ends in 6–8 weeks.

Keeping unhatched eggs

  • After nine days, collect eggs by suctioning the sediment (looks like tiny grains of brown sand).
  • Soak unhatched eggs in freshwater for two weeks.
  • After two weeks, filter and dry the eggs in sunlight. Store in a cool, dry place.
  • Eggs last up to one year, but hatch rate decreases over time.

Tips

  • If you run out of food, add 5–10 teaspoons of established aquarium water to feed them.
  • Newly hatched fairy shrimp and moina may be too small to see clearly.
  • If hatch rate is low, dry eggs and repeat the “unhatched eggs” process.
