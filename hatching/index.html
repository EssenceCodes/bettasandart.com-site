FAIRY SHRIMP & MOINA HATCHING INSTRUCTIONS

KEEP WATER TEMPERATURE BETWEEN 75–80°F DEGREES

Build your hatchery

Items you’ll need:

  • A lamp or similar direct light heat source
  • 3/16″ Rigid Airline Tubing (Length: 12″)
  • Two Water Bottles

Step 1

Cut bottles along the lines. You can repurpose the water inside the bottles to hatch your moina and fairy shrimp eggs. If using tap water, use a water conditioner to remove chlorine. These are freshwater invertebrates. DO NOT USE AQUARIUM SALT!

Step 2

Assemble hatchery:

  • Place one bottle upside down inside the other. The bottom one will serve as the base. Fill your hatchery with 1/3 of spring water. The weight will keep it stable.
  • Connect flexible air tubing to the air pump, then to the rigid air tubing.
  • Place rigid tubing inside your hatchery and turn on your air pump.

Step 3

Prepare and hatch the eggs:

  • Warm the eggs under sunlight or a light bulb for 2–4 hours. Then soak them in bottled spring water or dechlorinated freshwater for 8–24 hours.
  • Oxygenate the water with an air stone and turn on a lamp placed directly over the hatchery. Keep the light on until the eggs hatch.
  • Fairy shrimp hatch in 1–2 days. Moina hatch in 3–10 days (sometimes up to 16 if conditions are poor). Feed twice daily using dry yeast and/or spirulina — only add more food once previous food is eaten.
  • Keep water level stable by topping off with spring water. After 6–8 weeks, the colony will naturally die off.

Keeping unhatched eggs

  • After nine days, collect the new eggs by suctioning sediment from the bottom. The eggs look like tiny grains of brown sand.
  • Unhatched eggs must soak in freshwater for two weeks.
  • After two weeks, filter the eggs, dry them in sunlight, and store them in a zip-lock bag in a cool, dry place.
  • Eggs store for up to one year, but hatch rate declines over time.

Tips

  • If you run out of food, you can use established aquarium freshwater (5–10 teaspoons) to feed them.
  • Newly hatched fairy shrimp and moina are tiny and may be hard to see.
  • If hatch rate is low, dry the eggs and repeat the “unhatched eggs” method.
